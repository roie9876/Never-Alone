### 2. B2B Licensing (Secondary)

**Target:** Care homes, assisted living facilities, municipalities, healthcare providers

#### Enterprise Pricing:
- **Per-user licensing:** $15-20/user/month (volume discounts)
- **Site license:** $2,500-5,000/month for unlimited users at one facility
- **White-label option:** $10,000+ setup fee + per-user fees

#### B2B Value Proposition:
- Reduce caregiver burden
- Improve medication adherence
- Enhance resident satisfaction scores
- Lower costs (fewer ER visits from missed medications)
- Regulatory compliance support

#### Target Customers:
1. **Senior living facilities** (500+ residents)
2. **Home care agencies** (distribute tablets to clients)
3. **Municipal aging services** (subsidize for low-income seniors)
4. **Health insurance companies** (preventive care benefit)
5. **Hospital discharge programs** (reduce readmissions)

## Go-to-Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Pilot (Months 1-3)
**Goal:** Validate product with 10-20 families

**Tactics:**
- Recruit through local senior centers in Israel
- Free beta access in exchange for feedback
- Weekly check-ins with families
- Iterate based on user feedback

**Budget:** $10K (tablets, incentives, marketing)

---

### Phase 2: Soft Launch (Months 4-6)
**Goal:** Reach 100 paying users (Israel)

**Tactics:**
- Landing page + waitlist
- Content marketing (blog: "combating loneliness in seniors")
- Social media ads (Facebook, targeted to adult children 40-60)
- PR: Local Israeli media (tech + health journalists)
- Partnerships: Senior centers, home care agencies

**Channels:**
- Facebook/Instagram ads: $5K/month
- Google Ads (search: "dementia companion app"): $3K/month
- PR outreach: DIY
- Partnerships: Commission-based (10% referral fee)

**Budget:** $25K (ads + operations)

---

### Phase 3: Growth (Months 7-12)
**Goal:** 1,000 paying users (Israel + U.S. early adopters)

**Tactics:**
- Scale paid ads (Facebook, Google, YouTube)
- Influencer partnerships (senior care influencers, family vloggers)
- B2B pilot: 1-2 senior living facilities
- App Store Optimization (ASO)
- Referral program: "Give 1 month free, get 1 month free"

**Channels:**
- Paid ads: $20K/month
- B2B sales: 1 dedicated BD person
- Content marketing: SEO blog + YouTube

**Budget:** $150K (ads + team + operations)

---

### Phase 4: Scale (Year 2+)
**Goal:** 10,000+ paying users, enterprise deals

**Tactics:**
- National U.S. campaigns
- B2B focus: Healthcare partnerships (Medicare Advantage plans)
- Retail partnerships: Bundle with tablets (Best Buy, Apple Store)
- International expansion: UK, Canada, Australia

**Channels:**
- TV ads (targeted to seniors on daytime TV)
- Pharmacy partnerships (CVS, Walgreens displays)
- Insurance integrations (covered benefit)

   - Physical robot companion
   - **Pros:** Strong brand, B2B traction
   - **Cons:** Expensive ($250 device + $30/month), limited language support

   - **Pros:** Healthcare market fit
   - **Cons:** No conversational AI, less emotional focus

   - **Cons:** Not real-time conversation, no reminders
