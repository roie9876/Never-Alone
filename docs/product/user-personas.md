## Primary Users

### 1. üëµ Elderly Living Alone (Cognitively Healthy)

**Profile:**
- Age: 70-85
- Lives independently
- Children/family live far away
- Socially isolated, limited mobility
- Comfortable with basic technology (TV remote, simple phone)

**Key Needs:**
- Daily companionship and conversation
- Reminders for medications, meals, appointments
- Emotional support during lonely moments
- Connection to family members
- Daily structure and routine

**Pain Points:**
- Long hours with no one to talk to
- Forgetting to take medications
- Difficulty reaching out to family (don't want to be a burden)
- Loss of purpose and daily motivation

**How Never Alone Helps:**
- Initiates friendly check-ins throughout the day
- Provides medication and meal reminders
- Engages in meaningful conversations about interests
- Facilitates connection with family through updates

### 2. üß† People with Early Dementia / Alzheimer's

**Profile:**
- Age: 65-80
- Diagnosed with early-stage dementia or Alzheimer's
- May live alone or with a spouse
- Experiencing memory challenges and confusion
- Need gentle guidance and reassurance

**Key Needs:**
- Simple, clear communication
- Frequent orientation (time, place, routine)
- Medication reminders with verification
- Reassurance when confused or anxious
- Gentle redirection from repetitive thoughts
- Preservation of dignity and independence

**Pain Points:**
- Anxiety from confusion and memory loss
- Forgetting to take medications (safety risk)
- Disorientation about time, place, or routine
- Fear and frustration from cognitive decline
- Feeling like a burden to family

**How Never Alone Helps:**
- **Dementia Mode**: AI initiates all conversations (no wake word to remember)
- Uses simple language and short, clear sentences
- Provides gentle orientation ("Today is Monday, November 9th")
- Medication reminders with friendly follow-up
- Reassuring tone during moments of confusion
- Big visual buttons and minimal complexity

### 4. üíô Anyone Experiencing Loneliness

**Profile:**
- Age: Any (25-85+)

- Craves human connection and understanding

- Broader conversation topics (hobbies, feelings, memories)

## User Journey Mapping

### Example: Daily Journey for Elderly User with Dementia

**Morning (8:00 AM)**
- Device greets user: "Good morning! It's Monday, November 9th. Did you sleep well?"
- Reminds about breakfast and morning medication

**Mid-Morning (10:30 AM)**
- Initiates conversation: "Would you like to look at some family photos?"
- Discusses photos, recalls memories together

**Afternoon (2:00 PM)**
- Reminds about lunch and afternoon medication
- Asks about feelings: "How are you feeling today?"

**Evening (6:00 PM)**
- Dinner reminder
- Family call notification: "Sarah wanted to call you this evening"

**Night (9:00 PM)**
- Gentle wind-down: "It's getting late. How about we get ready for bed?"

## Design Implications

### For Elderly (Cognitively Healthy)
- Natural conversation flow with wake word option
- Rich conversation topics
- Calendar and appointment integration
- Photo sharing and memory features

### For Dementia/Alzheimer's Users
- **No wake word required** (AI initiates)
- Large visual "Talk to Me" button
- Simple, repetitive language patterns
- Frequent orientation cues
- Calming tone and pace
- Minimal visual complexity

### For Family Dashboard
- Quick daily summary view
- Alert system for important events
- Easy photo/reminder updates
- Trend visualization (mood, engagement)
- Privacy controls and settings
